Electronic Arts (EA) came out with quarterly earnings of $1.46 per share, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $1.27 per share. This compares to earnings of $1.28 per share a year ago. These figures are adjusted for non-recurring items.
This quarterly report represents an earnings surprise of 14.96%. A quarter ago, it was expected that this video game maker would post earnings of $1.01 per share when it actually produced earnings of $1.14, delivering a surprise of 12.87%.
Over the last four quarters, the company has surpassed consensus EPS estimates three times.
Electronic Arts , which belongs to the Zacks Toys - Games - Hobbies industry, posted revenues of $1.82 billion for the quarter ended September 2023, surpassing the Zacks Consensus Estimate by 2.86%. This compares to year-ago revenues of $1.75 billion. The company has topped consensus revenue estimates two times over the last four quarters.
The sustainability of the stock's immediate price movement based on the recently-released numbers and future earnings expectations will mostly depend on management's commentary on the earnings call.
Electronic Arts shares have added about 1.3% since the beginning of the year versus the S&P 500's gain of 9.2%.
