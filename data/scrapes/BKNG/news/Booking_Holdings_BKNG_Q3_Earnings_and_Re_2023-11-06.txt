Booking Holdings (BKNG) came out with quarterly earnings of $72.32 per share, beating the Zacks Consensus Estimate of $67.86 per share. This compares to earnings of $53.03 per share a year ago. These figures are adjusted for non-recurring items.
This quarterly report represents an earnings surprise of 6.57%. A quarter ago, it was expected that this online booking service would post earnings of $28.84 per share when it actually produced earnings of $37.62, delivering a surprise of 30.44%.
Over the last four quarters, the company has surpassed consensus EPS estimates four times.
Booking Holdings , which belongs to the Zacks Internet - Commerce industry, posted revenues of $7.34 billion for the quarter ended September 2023, surpassing the Zacks Consensus Estimate by 1.54%. This compares to year-ago revenues of $6.05 billion. The company has topped consensus revenue estimates four times over the last four quarters.
The sustainability of the stock's immediate price movement based on the recently-released numbers and future earnings expectations will mostly depend on management's commentary on the earnings call.
Booking Holdings shares have added about 37.3% since the beginning of the year versus the S&P 500's gain of 10.4%.
